Shoyu Ramen Recipe by Keizo Shimamoto

Credits: Way of Ramen

Equipment Needed

  • Knife
  • 12-quart pots
  • Ladle
  • Jar containers
  • Cooking Pot
  • Bowls
  • Scissors
  • Strainer
  • Spoon (preferably wooden)
  • Thermometer
  • Tongs
  • Measuring Cup

Ingredients

Tare:

  • 600ml Marudaizu Koikuchi Shoyu (丸大豆　醤油)
  • 65ml Hon Mirin
  • 10g Aged Sea Salt
  • 20ml Thai Fish Sauce

Chicken Stock:

  • 2 Whole Chickens (10lbs)
  • 5lbs Chicken Feet
  • 8-9L Water
  • Large Handful of Chicken skin
  • 1/2 Head Napa Cabbage
  • 1 Bulb of Garlic (cut in half)
  • 50g Ginger (Peeled and sliced as thinly as possible)

Dashi:

  • 5L Water
  • 50g Ma Konbu
  • 25g Dried Shiitake
  • 65g Katsuobushi

Shallot Oil:

  • 250ml Vegetable Oil
  • 100g Shallots
  • 25g Ginger
  • 25g Garlic
  • 25g Green Onions

Final Bowl:

  • 100ml Dashi
  • 200ml Chicken Stock
  • 20-22ml Tare
  • 10ml Chiyu
  • 5-10ml Shallot Oil

Toppings:

  • Chashu
  • spinach
  • menma
  • green onions
  • narutomaki
  • age negi
  • nori

Steps

  1. Start with the tare first
  2. Combine 600ml of Marudaizu Koikuchi Shoyu with 6ml of Hon Mirin. Let it sit at room temperature for at least 1 week.
  3. After a week, add 20g of kombu and 2 shitake mushrooms into the blend
  4. Put the tare mixture into a pot and heat it to 80°C (176°F) , then remove the kombu and shitake
  5. Add 10g of the best tasting natural salt you can find
  6. Stir the salt in and let it dissolve with the residual heat
  7. Let the mixture cool down then add 20ml of Thai Fish Sauce

    8. The Tare can be stored in a container for a very long time


  8. Afterwards, make with the soup
  9. Rinse off 5 pounds (2.5 kilograms) of chicken feet
  10. Trim off the toenails using scissors and cut an X into the foot
  11. Reach into the chicken cavities and remove all the guts. Then rinse the chicken
  12. Make cuts all around the chicken with a knife to extract more flavor when creating the stock
  13. Place the chickens into a large pot and add 8-9 liters of water. The quantity depends on pot size.
  14. Turn on heat and when the scum starts to clump up on top, skim it off.
  15. Stick a long spoon (preferably the wooden one) into the chickens cavity to remove any stuck scum inside and remove that also
  16. Add Chicken feet and a large handful of chicken skin
  17. Lower heat and bring the stock to around 95°C (203°F) , keep the stock just under boiling
  18. Place a lid on the pot but keep it slightly cracked
  19. Let the stock cook for a minimum of 6 hours, make sure it never boils
  20. Then, make the Dashi
  21. Soak 50g of kombu and 25g of shitake mushrooms into a pot with 5L of water.
  22. Let it soak overnight in a fridge
  23. On the next day, place the pot onto a stove and heat it to 60°C (140°F)
  24. When it hits 60°c (140°F) , remove the kombu and shitake mushrooms, then bring the pot to 90°C (194°F)
  25. Skim off any scum that appears
  26. Add a bag of katsuobushi when the dashi reaches 90°C (194°F) and turn off the heat
  27. Let the katsuobushi steep in the liquid
  28. Taste the katsuobushi after 5 minutes to see if it has a smokey fishy flavor. If it is over steeped, it will taste sour which is not good
  29. Strain out the dashi into a pot or container
  30. Finally, create the aroma oil
  31. Finely dice 100g of shallots
  32. Finely mince 25g of garlic, ginger, and the white part of green onions
  33. Heat up 250ml of vegetable oil and add all the aromatics
  34. Fry the aromatics at medium low heat until all the moisture has evaporated until a beautiful golden brown (make sure to constantly stir and do not let it burn, which it will easily do) . The oil should have a nice toasty oil aroma
  35. Strain out the oil into a container and put the aromatics onto a paper towel lined plate. The fried aromatics will become another topping for the ramen.
  36. Use a tiny ladle and skim the chicken fat on the surface of the broth into a container.
  37. Afterwards, strain out the soup into a pot separate from the chicken pieces.
  38. Then, cut up half a head of Nappa cabbage into 2-inch squares.
  39. Cut a bulb of garlic in half.
  40. Cut 50g of peeled ginger lengthwise and thinly as possible.
  41. Bring the strained soup to 95°C and add the cut aromatics into the pot.
  42. Steep the soup for 15 minutes while keeping it over 90°C and then strain it.

  43. Combine and heat up 100ml of dashi and 200ml of chicken stock in a pot.
  44. Then into a serving bowl, add 20ml of the tare, and 10ml of the chicken fat (chiyu) .
  45. When the soup is starting to simmer, cook the noodles in a separate pot of water.
  46. Add the soup into the bowl before the noodles finish cooking.
  47. When the noodles are done, strain them and add them to the bowl.
  48. Add toppings of your choice on and finish with aroma oil. (recommended toppings: Chashu, Spinach, Menma (pickled bamboo) , Green Onions, Age Negi (I don’t know what this is) , Narutomaki (fishcake) , and Nori (seaweed))
